Influenza Seasonal influenza occurs worldwide. The flu season usually runs from November to April in the northern hemisphere, between April and October in the southern hemisphere and year round in the tropics. Influenza (flu) is caused by a virus spread from person to person when they cough or sneeze or by touching objects and surfaces that have been contaminated with the virus. Get the flu shot. Measles: outbreak Outbreaks of measles are ongoing. Measles is a highly contagious viral disease that can cause serious complications for some people. You are at increased risk of measles infection if you have not had the illness or if you are not up to date on your vaccinations.
